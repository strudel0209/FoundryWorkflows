# Contoso Corp — Vendor Procurement Policy
**Document Version:** 3.2  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Owner:** Chief Procurement Officer  
**Classification:** Internal Use Only

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes standards and controls for selecting, onboarding, and managing vendors to ensure Contoso Corp's procurement activities comply with legal, ethical, financial, and operational requirements.

---

## 2. Scope

Applies to all procurement activities exceeding **USD 10,000** in total contract value across all business units globally.

---

## 3. Vendor Classification Tiers

| Tier | Annual Spend | Risk Profile | Approval Required |
|------|-------------|--------------|-------------------|
| Tier 1 — Strategic | > USD 1,000,000 | High | CPO + CFO + Legal |
| Tier 2 — Preferred | USD 100,000 – 1,000,000 | Medium | Department VP + Procurement |
| Tier 3 — Standard | USD 10,000 – 99,999 | Low | Procurement Manager |
| Tier 4 — Spot | < USD 10,000 | Minimal | Department Manager |

---

## 4. Mandatory Vendor Screening Requirements

Before any vendor may be onboarded, the following checks **must** be completed and documented:

### 4.1 Blacklist & Sanctions Check
- All vendors must be screened against the **Contoso Vendor Blacklist** (maintained by EthicsBoard).
- All vendors must be checked against **OFAC SDN List**, **EU Consolidated Sanctions List**, and **UN Security Council Sanctions List**.
- Any positive match results in **automatic rejection** with no exceptions.

### 4.2 Financial Viability Check
- Vendors must demonstrate a **Debt-to-Equity Ratio ≤ 2.0** for Tier 1 and Tier 2 vendors.
- **Current Ratio** (Current Assets / Current Liabilities) must be **≥ 1.2** for all tiers.
- **Credit Score** must be **≥ 650** for standard onboarding. Scores between 600–649 require CFO waiver.
- Vendors with **negative net income for 2 consecutive years** are classified as High Risk.

### 4.3 Ethical & Compliance Standards
- All vendors must acknowledge receipt of Contoso's **Supplier Code of Conduct**.
- Tier 1 and Tier 2 vendors must provide evidence of **ISO 9001** or equivalent certification.
- Vendors operating in high-risk geographies (per the Contoso Country Risk Registry) require additional due diligence including on-site audit or third-party audit report.

### 4.4 Data Security Requirements
- Vendors handling Contoso data must complete a **Data Security Assessment (DSA)**.
- Cloud/SaaS vendors must provide **SOC 2 Type II** report dated within the last 18 months.

---

## 5. Risk Scoring Methodology

Procurement uses a **composite risk score** (0–100, lower is riskier) calculated from:

| Factor | Weight |
|--------|--------|
| Financial health score | 30% |
| Compliance & ethics | 25% |
| Operational history | 20% |
| Market reputation | 15% |
| Geographic risk | 10% |

**Risk Tiers:**  
- **Score 75–100:** Low Risk → auto-approve for Tier 3/4  
- **Score 50–74:** Medium Risk → standard approval flow  
- **Score 25–49:** High Risk → requires CPO + Human approval  
- **Score 0–24:** Critical Risk → auto-reject  

---

## 6. Human Approval Requirements

The following scenarios **always require Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) approval** regardless of automated scores:

1. Overall risk score **below 50**.
2. Vendor classified as operating in a **Restricted Country** (See Appendix A).
3. Any vendor with a **prior blacklist entry** that has been appealed and reinstated.
4. Contract value exceeding **USD 500,000** for any single engagement.
5. Vendor supplying **critical components** defined in the Bill of Materials Risk Register.

---

## 7. Contract Requirements

All vendor agreements must include:
- Net payment terms (Net 30 default; Net 60 requires VP approval)
- Intellectual property indemnification clause
- Data protection addendum (GDPR / CCPA compliant)
- Right to audit clause (annual, with 30 days notice)
- Force majeure and business continuity provisions
- Termination for convenience (90-day notice)

---

## 8. Violations & Consequences

Procurement violations are classified as:
- **Minor:** Process deviation without financial impact → Corrective Action Plan
- **Moderate:** Financial impact < USD 50,000 → Department head accountable + review
- **Major:** Financial impact ≥ USD 50,000 or legal exposure → Disciplinary action + legal escalation

## Appendix A — Restricted Countries (Requiring Enhanced Due Diligence)

Belarus, Cuba, Iran, Myanmar, North Korea, Russia, Syria, Venezuela, Zimbabwe.
